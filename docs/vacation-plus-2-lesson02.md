# Vacation Plus 2 - Lesson 02: Eating Out

## Informações da Lição

- **Livro:** Vacation Plus 2
- **Lição:** 02
- **Tema:** Eating Out (Comendo Fora)
- **Duração Total:** ~50 minutos (1 hora de aula)

## Objetivos da Lição

- Restaurant vocabulary (Vocabulário de restaurante)
- Talking about favorite places to eat (Falar sobre lugares favoritos para comer)
- Food (Comida)
- Would like x Want
- "Feel like" and "can't stand"

---

## Seção 1: Do You Remember?

**Tempo sugerido:** 10 minutos  
**Tipo:** Group work

### Itens Lexicais para Revisão

| Expressões de Restaurante | Expressões de Comida | Vocabulário |
|--------------------------|---------------------|-------------|
| Could we have a table for...? | a wonderful meal | salad dressing |
| Would you like to sit...? | No, thanks, I'm full. | napkin |
| by the window | Would you like another...? | silverware |
| Are you ready to order? | to fix a meal | appetizer |
| I'll have... | That's it for now. | main course |
| How would you like your steak? | to feel like [eating out]* | a three-course meal |
| rare | to take [your] order | spicy |
| medium | to have for dessert | bitter |
| well-done | I can't stand [eating spinach]* | sour |
| What would you like to drink? | Could you bring us...? | to have a barbecue |

### Estruturas Gramaticais: "Feel like" e "Can't stand"

**Regra:** Quando um verbo segue as expressões marcadas com *, ele deve ser escrito e pronunciado com **-ing**.

| Exemplos com "feel like" | Exemplos com "can't stand" |
|-------------------------|---------------------------|
| I feel like **having** chocolate cake. | I can't stand **eating** salad every day. |
| We feel like **drinking** wine tonight. | He can't stand **drinking** wine. |
| Do you feel like **having** dessert? | We can't stand **eating** spicy food. |
| He doesn't feel like **eating** pasta tonight. | She can't stand **going** there anymore. |
| Does she feel like **having** ice cream for dessert? | |
| He feels like **eating** out today. | |
| She feels like **cooking** us a meal. | |

### Dicas para Alunos

- **Ao pedir comida/bebida:** I'll have..., I'd like..., I want...
- **Para recusar:** No thanks, I'm full / No thanks, I'm fine = Não obrigado, estou satisfeito.

## Seção 2: Putting Into Practice

**Tempo sugerido:** 10 minutos  
**Tipo:** Group work / Pair work

### Atividade 01: Diálogo - O que você quer para o jantar?

**Contexto:** Conversa entre marido e mulher (Martha e Rafael) sobre o jantar.

| Personagem | Fala | Resposta |
|-----------|------|----------|
| **Martha** | What do you **want** for dinner tonight? | |
| **Rafael** | I don't know. Would you like to go out? | |
| **Martha** | No, we eat out all the time. I'd like to stay home tonight. | |
| **Rafael** | What do you want for dinner tonight? | |
| **Martha** | Uh... no, we need to go to the grocery store. And we don't have any vegetables, either. | |
| **Rafael** | So, I guess we have to get some. And we don't have any in the freezer? | |
| **Martha** | Ok. Well, so... I'd like some chicken. Do we have any in the freezer? | |
| **Rafael** | Hmmm. I have another idea. Let's just go out for dinner! | |

**Perguntas para discussão:**
1. What does Rafael want for dinner?
2. Does Martha want to go out?
3. Do they have chicken?
4. What else do they need?
5. What are they going to do?

**Instruções para o instrutor:**
- First, ask a few students the question: "What do you want for dinner tonight?"
- Second, have the class form pairs in order to read the conversation between a husband and wife, Rafael and Martha
- Say "Find the answer to the question 'What does Rafael want for dinner?'"
- Ask them what the answer is – "Rafael wants some chicken."
- Now find a sentence that would like is a polite way to say want
- "Now look at the conversation and find a question that means 'Do you want to go out?' [would you like to go out?]"
- Remind students that would like is a polite way to say want
- Next, have pairs practice the conversation, taking turns playing each role
- Practice the conversation using their own ideas

## Seção 3: Now, It's Your Turn!

**Tempo sugerido:** 20 minutos  
**Tipo:** Group work

### Atividade: Discussão sobre Restaurantes Favoritos

**Tema:** Tell your classmates about your favorite restaurant.

**Perguntas guia:**
- What's your favorite restaurant?
- Where is it?
- What kind of food do they serve?
- What do you usually order there?
- How often do you go there?
- Who do you usually go with?
