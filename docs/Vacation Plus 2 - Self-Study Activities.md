## Lesson 01 - GOING ON VACATION

### 1) Complete the Conversations

**a) Viagem ao México**
> A – Say, _________________ to Mexico?  
> *(como foi sua viagem)*  
> B – Oh, it was terrific. I enjoyed it a lot.  
> A – _________________?  
> *(Quanto tempo você ficou lá?)*  
> B – I stayed there for two weeks.  
> A – _________________?  
> *(Você tirou muitas fotos?)*  
> B – Oh yes, a lot.

**b) Viagem ao Rio de Janeiro**
> A – I went to Rio de Janeiro on my vacation.  
> B – _________________?  
> *(O que você achou do Rio?)*  
> A – I enjoyed it a lot. Rio is a beautiful city to visit.  
> B – _________________ there?  
> *(Como estava o tempo)*  
> A – It was sunny and hot most of the time.  
> B – _________________?  
> *(Você ficou lá o tempo todo?)*  
> A – No, actually I went to Búzios on the weekend.

**c) Férias na praia**
> A – _________________?  
> *(Como você geralmente passa as férias?)*  
> B – I usually go to the beach with my family.

**d) Piquenique**
> A – _________________?  
> *(Por que nós não fazemos um piquenique e pescamos também?)*  
> B – That's a terrific idea!

### 2) Make Up Questions

Formule perguntas usando a palavra em negrito:

| Palavra | Resposta | Pergunta |
|---------|----------|----------|
| 1 – Where | She wants to take a picture by the river. | Where does she want to take a picture? |
| 2 – When | He traveled to Natal last weekend. | When did he travel to Natal? |
| 3 – Why | That's a great idea! You know I enjoy going fishing. | Why is that a great idea? |
| 4 – Where | He usually goes camping at the beach. | Where does he usually go camping? |
| 5 – What | It was cloudy and cool. | What was the weather like? |

### 3) Make Up Sentences Using "Enjoy"

| Palavras | Frase |
|----------|-------|
| a) She (travel / her vacation) | She enjoys traveling on her vacation. |
| b) We (go camping / at the beach) | We enjoy going camping at the beach. |
| c) I (take pictures / flowers) | I enjoy taking pictures of flowers. |

## Lesson 02 - EATING OUT

### 1) Fill in the Gaps

Complete as frases com as palavras corretas:

**a)** A – Do you want _______ before your _______?  
*(Você quer um aperitivo antes do seu prato principal?)*  
B – No, thanks. I _______ and it's already _______.  
*(Não obrigado. Eu vou querer massa e filé e já é pesado.)*

**b)** A – Let's _______ on Saturday?  
*(Vamos fazer um churrasco no sábado?)*  
B – Great idea! I _______ meat.  
*(Ótima ideia. Eu estou com vontade de comer carne.)*

**c)** A – What _______?  
*(O que você pediu?)*  
B – I _______ Mexican food.  
*(Eu pedi comida mexicana.)*  
A – Oh, but I _______ it. It's _______.  
*(Oh, mas eu odeio. É picante demais)*

**d)** A – How do you _______?  
*(Como você gosta do seu filé?)*  
B – I prefer it _______ please.  
*(Eu prefiro ele malpassado, por favor.)*

**e)** A – Do you _______ to eat?  
*(Você está com vontade de sair pra comer?)*  
B – Actually, I prefer to cook _______ to eat.  
*(Na realidade, eu prefiro cozinhar ao invés de sair para comer.)*

**f)** A – _______ Japanese food?  
*(Você gostaria de comer comida japonesa?)*  
B – Sorry but I _______ it.  
*(Desculpe, mas eu não suporto.)*  
_______ Italian food if you don't mind.  
*(Eu gostaria de comer comida italiana se você não se importa.)*

### 2) Agree with the Statements

Concorde com as afirmações seguindo o exemplo:

| Afirmação | Resposta |
|-----------|----------|
| I like Italian food. | So do I. |
| I'm very hungry. | So am I. |
| I feel like having a barbecue. | So do I. |
| I don't like the taste of sweetener. | Neither do I. |
| I can't stand greasy food. | Neither can I. |
| I prefer my steak well done. | So do I. |
| I won't have dessert. | Neither will I. |
| We had a salad for dinner. | So did we. |
| She can cook very well. | So can she. |
| I didn't order coke. | Neither did I. |
| I love having ice cream. | So do I. |
| I can't decide what to order. | Neither can I. |

## Lesson 03 - AROUND TOWN

### 1) Fill in the Gaps

Complete as frases sobre direções:

**a)** A – Excuse me, _______ a gas station _______?  
*(Com licença, tem um posto de gasolina por aqui?)*  
B – Yes, _______ opposite the supermarket.  
*(Sim, tem um em frente ao supermercado.)*

**b)** A – Can you help me _______?  
*(Você pode me ajudar a achar este endereço?)*  
B – It's very easy. It's just _______ next to the church.  
*(É muito fácil. Fica apenas a duas quadras daqui ao lado da igreja.)*

**c)** A – I'm _______. Are there any _______?  
*(Estou procurando uma banca de jornal. Tem alguma perto daqui?)*  
B – Yes, _______ the next bus stop.  
*(Sim, tem uma perto do próximo ponto de ônibus.)*

**d)** A – How do _______?  
*(Como eu chego até o museu?)*  
B – _______ the street and _______ over there _______, and you'll see it _______.  
*(Desça a rua e no semáforo logo ali vire à direita. Ande um pouco e você verá na sua esquerda.)*

**e)** A – Do you _______ the travel agency is?  
*(Você sabe onde é a agência de turismo?)*  
B – Yes, go up the street and turn left. It's right _______.  
*(Sim, suba a rua duas quadras e vire à esquerda. É bem na esquina.)*

### 2) Ask Questions Using "How long does/did/will it take...?"

| Informação | Pergunta | Resposta |
|------------|----------|----------|
| (Sarah / read a book / two weeks) | How long does it take Sarah to read a book? | It takes her around two weeks. |
| (you / take a shower / 10 minutes) | How long does it take you to take a shower? | It takes me around 10 minutes. |
| (Richard / get to work / 30 minutes by car) | How long does it take Richard to get to work? | It takes him around 30 minutes by car. |
| (Julie and I / go downtown by taxi / will take around 20 minutes) | How long will it take Julie and I to go downtown by taxi? | It will take around 20 minutes. |
| (your parents / to arrive in Miami / will take around 9 hours) | How long will it take your parents to arrive in Miami? | It will take around 9 hours. |
| (Ann / walked to school / 45 minutes) | How long did it take Ann to walk to school? | It took her around 45 minutes. |
| (Julie / found a place to live / three weeks) | How long did it take Julie to find a place to live? | It took her around three weeks. |

## Lesson 04 - TALKING ABOUT OTHERS

### 1) Translate the Sentences into English

| Português | English |
|-----------|---------|
| a) Minha cunhada é de veneta. Eu nunca sei quando falar com ela. | My sister-in-law is moody. I never know when to talk to her. |
| b) Eu não consigo acreditar que ela vai se casar com aquele cara. | I can't believe she's going to marry that guy. |
| c) Embora eu esteja zangada com ele, eu o convidei para minha festa. | Although I'm angry with him, I invited him to my party. |
| d) Eles se divorciaram. Ela disse que ele é muito ciumento e teimoso. | They got divorced. She said he's very jealous and stubborn. |
| e) Ela é muito sensível e fica chateada facilmente com todos. | She's very sensitive and gets upset easily with everyone. |
| f) É difícil fazer amizade com ele, pois ele é muito quieto. | It's hard to make friends with him because he's very quiet. |
| g) Ela noivou. É por isso que ela está de tão bom humor. | She got engaged. That's why she's in such a good mood. |
| h) Ela estava de mau humor. Ela nem sequer disse oi para mim. | She was in a bad mood. She didn't even say hi to me. |
| i) Ele é muito esquecido. Ele nem sequer lembra o nome dela. | He's very forgetful. He doesn't even remember her name. |
| j) Ela sorri para todo mundo. | She smiles at everyone. |

### 2) Match the Sentences

| Situação | Resposta |
|----------|----------|
| 1) Would you like to go out to dance tonight? | I'd love to. It's very kind of you to say that. |
| 2) Do you know Mark? | Sure! We became friends when we were kids. |
| 3) Did you know I got engaged to Laura? | Really? And when are you getting married? |
| 4) I can't stand her anymore. | Neither can I. She's two-faced, so it's not easy to be friends with her. |
| 5) I'm feeling weird in this dress. I think I'm going to change. | Why? You look so neat in it. |
| 6) You're so friendly and polite. Why don't we get together again on Saturday night? | I'm sorry but I'm not really in the mood. |
